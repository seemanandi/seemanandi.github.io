Research Projects

Bacteriocin Research

Study On Pathogenic Bacteria Inhibition Mechanism by Lactobacillus sp. Bacteriocin (2022)

Objective: Determine the susceptibility of different pathogenic bacteria to the inhibitory effects of Lactobacillus-derived bacteriocins.

This research focuses on investigating the inhibition mechanism of pathogenic bacteria by bacteriocins produced by Lactobacillus species. The study aims to understand the specific interactions and processes through which Lactobacillus-derived bacteriocins inhibit the growth and proliferation of pathogenic bacteria, contributing to the development of potential alternatives to traditional antibiotics.

Molecular Biology Antimicrobial Research Lactobacillus

Plant-Microbe Interaction

Phosphate Solubilizing Rhizobacteria Study (2018-2019)

Objective: To enhance agricultural yield through beneficial plant-microbe interactions.

This study deals with the isolation of phosphate-solubilizing rhizobacteria from paddy plant rhizosphere and in-vitro screening of different plant growth-promoting activities like NH3, HCN, catalase production, and stress tolerance. The research contributes to sustainable agriculture practices.

Plant Pathology Agricultural Microbiology Sustainable Agriculture
